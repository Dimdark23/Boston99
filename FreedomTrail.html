Freedom Trail

The Freedom Trail in Boston is a 2.5-mile-long trail that passes by 16 historically significant sites, including museums, meetinghouses, churches, and burial grounds. The trail tells the story of Boston's role in the American Revolution and the struggle for independence. Visiting the Freedom Trail can teach you about the events and people that shaped the United States of America. Some famous sites along the Freedom Trail include the Massachusetts State House, the Old North Church, and the USS Constitution Museum. It's a great way to experience American history and learn about the country's origins.
